Frequently Asked Questions

1. When was Edward Hopper born?

Hopper was born in 1882 in Upper Nyack, New York.

2. When did Hopper begin painting?

His parents encouraged his interest in art from an early age, ang Hopper completed his first oil painting at age 13.

3. What other interests did Hopper have?

While in high school, Hopper expressed interest in becoming a naval architect. Though he ultimately chose to focus on his art, his passion for nautical scenes is evident in his work.

4. Did anyone inspire or influence Hopper's art?

Hopper often read and reread the works of Ralph Waldo Emerson, and Emerson's philosophy influenced Hopper's life and self-image. Hopper also imitated the works of French painters at the beginning of his career.

5. Where did Hopper study art?

He attended the New York School of Art and Design, which would later become The New School for Design.

6. How did Hopper earn a living before he became well-known?

Thanks to his parents' insistence that he study commercial art, Hopper worked for an advertising agency after graduating in 1905. He crafted various magazine covers and designs, but hated such work.

7. When did Hopper sell his first painting?

In 1913, Hopper sold "Sailing" for $250, which was painted over a previous self-portrait.

8. Was Hopper married?

In 1924, Hopper married Josephine Nivison, a woman he originally met in art school. She ended her art career in order to become Hopper's manager.

9. Where did Hopper find subjects for his paintings?

Hopper was fascinated by the landscape of New England, and most of his works were done there. However, Hopper was also taken with darker, urban scenes as well, and his home in Manhattan facilitated the creation of his more urban works.

10. When did Hopper die?

Hopper passed away in his studio in 1967 after gaining major recognition for his art. His collection was donated to the Whitney Museum of American Art.
